Results

Frequency analysis was done on the orange, red, and blue line as well as the commuter rail. Fourier transformations were done on each data set and the highest amplitude frequencies were extracted from the data and put into graphs, with their respective peaks being found.

Overview

Below are pictures of both low frequency and high frequency transformations for both the low-frequency vibrations and high-frequency vibrations. Two different data collections were done for low-frequency versus high-frequency data, as it allowed for more exact data collection and ease in analyzing and transforming data.

Vibration Analysis

Below are pictures of both low frequency and high frequency transformations for both the low-frequency vibrations and high-frequency vibrations. Two different data collections were done for low-frequency versus high-frequency data, as it allowed for more exact data collection and ease in analyzing and transforming data.

  • Red Line: There was a significant peak at 47Hz.
  • Orange Line: There was a significant peak at 10Hz, 14Hz, and 47Hz.
  • Blue Line: There was a significant peak at 43Hz and 47 Hz.
  • Commuter Rail: There was also a significant peak at 3Hz and 23Hz.
